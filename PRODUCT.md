# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indian citizens aged 18–45 who need to report civic issues — potholes, broken streetlights, water leakage, garbage overflow — quickly and from their phone. They're frustrated, time-pressed, and sceptical that complaints go anywhere. They need speed, clarity, and visible proof that their report was received and is being acted on.

Secondary users: municipal workers and local government staff who triage, assign, and resolve incoming reports.

## Tagline

"See it. Report it. Fix it."

## Mission

Empower every citizen to become a guardian of their city — using AI to turn ignored complaints into accountable action.

## Product Purpose

Jaagruk is an AI-powered civic intelligence platform where citizens report local issues with photos, and a multi-agent AI pipeline automatically validates, classifies, routes to the right authority, and generates official complaints — turning passive frustration into structured, trackable civic action.

Success looks like: a citizen spots a pothole, snaps a photo, drops a pin, submits in under 60 seconds. The AI validates the image, classifies the issue (road damage / water / sanitation / electrical), routes it to the correct municipal department, and generates a formal complaint with a trackable ID. The citizen sees real-time status updates as the complaint moves through the system.

## Brand Personality

Urgent. Trustworthy. Empowering. Intelligent.

Voice is direct and action-oriented — not bureaucratic, not preachy. The interface should feel like a tool that takes you seriously: fast feedback, clear status, zero ambiguity. Confidence comes from speed and transparency, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Generic government portals** — slow, ugly, confusing forms, broken layouts, no feedback. The opposite of trust.
- **Overly corporate SaaS dashboards** — cold, impersonal, metric-obsessed. Jaagruk serves people, not KPIs.
- Activist / protest aesthetics (aggressive reds, fists, all-caps shouting). This is civic infrastructure, not a campaign.
- Cluttered news sites with ad chaos.

## Design Principles

1. **60-second reporting** — Every design decision should reduce friction on the submission path. If it slows the report, cut it.
2. **Status is the interface** — The most important screen is the one that shows "your complaint was heard, here's what's happening." Visibility of system status above all.
3. **Proof over promise** — Show evidence: photos, timestamps, assignment logs, resolution updates. Don't just say "we'll handle it."
4. **Respect the user's time** — Mobile-first, low-bandwidth-aware, no unnecessary steps. Citizens are reporting problems in the field, often frustrated.
5. **Civic trust through craft** — Premium build quality signals that the system is competent. Linear-grade polish applied to public infrastructure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AAA compliance — this is a government-facing public tool; maximum accessibility is non-negotiable.
- Mobile-first: designed for one-handed use on mid-range Android phones.
- Low-bandwidth aware: works on 3G connections, images compress before upload.
- Hindi and English at minimum; architecture should support multilingual expansion.
- High contrast, large touch targets (≥48px), reduced motion support.
- Screen reader and keyboard navigation fully supported.
